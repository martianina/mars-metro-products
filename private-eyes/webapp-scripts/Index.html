Private Eyes 4: GDPR & More
Do you collect personal information from users in Europe, United Kingdom, Iceland, Switzerland, Norway or Liechtenstein?

The General Data Privacy Directive (GDPR) protects the data of citizens in these regions.

If you collect personally identifiable information, GDPR requires that you obtain consent from users, as well as keep a system for tracking and removing consent. If you are not sure how the GDPR affects you, we recommend you consult an attorney.
The GDPR requires you have a system in place to protect data and deal with breaches. GDPR also requires you to designate accountable parties and review vendor contracts for their data processing practices. If you have not done this, we recommend you seek a data security consultation.
Collecting data from minors

GDPR, U.S. Federal law, and some state require special treatment of the personal data of minors. The GDPR requires that sites get parental consent from children under 16, but individual states can reduce the age to as low as 13. U.S. Child Online Privacy & Protection Act (COPPA), requires parental consent from users under 13. Some states, for example, California and Delaware, have enacted privacy protections for minors. The consent rules for minors are complex. If you collect or use the data of children under 16, we recommend you consult a data privacy attorney. For more information view the following video.
